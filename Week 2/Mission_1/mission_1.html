Excited: “Yeah! I’m excited to tackle this new challenge. Let’s go!”

"Hopeful: “Ok, let’s serve this app up/ npm start/ press play/ compile etc. Fingers crossed this works!”

Confused: “Wait what? Why is the code mad at me? What’s all this red in the console? Alright, time to debug! Let’s Google, use Stack Overflow. Ask friends (in my case, classmates at Nashville Software School).”

Frustrated: “I’m banging my head against the proverbial wall. I’ve tried everything and anything. I’m in a mental cycle, circling around possible solutions with no exit in sight.

Anxious: “I’m starting to panic. Do I even know what I am doing? Will I graduate this bootcamp? What if I don’t pass ? When will I ever feel confident that I know how to code? What if I don’t get a job?”

Despair/Shame: “Maybe I’m terrible at this. How is (insert classmate/colleague) getting it right and not panicking? I hate my life, I hate myself. Why can’t I figure this out?!?!? I’ve exhausted my resources and my brain. I’m intellectually fried.”

Jubilation/elation: “WHAT!??! IT WORKED?!?!? I CANNOT BELIEVE IT! HALLELUJAH! I AM SO AWESOME. CODING IS PURE MAGIC. I LOVE THIS CAREER!”

Proud: “I’m so proud of myself and my work/ my team. I cannot believe I wrote all this code! Look at all this beautiful work. I’m gonna show it off.”

Gratitude: “Imposter syndrome is real. I’m so thankful for the dev community and everyone who helped me figure this out. I’m grateful I chose this path of learning software development. I’ll be kinder to myself next time; I’ve just got to be patient! Onto the next ticket…”
